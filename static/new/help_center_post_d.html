How to interpret Statistical Significance in AI Tests

Understanding confidence intervals, p-values, and automated decision-making in your A/B experiments.

AD
Alex D.
Updated 2 days ago 5 min read

Statistical significance is crucial for determining if your A/B test results are due to a specific change or just random chance. Our AI models automatically calculate this for you, but understanding the underlying mechanics can help you make better, more strategic decisions for your product roadmap.

Why Significance Matters

When running an experiment, you are essentially taking a sample of your user base to predict how the entire population would react. Without significance testing, you might mistake random noise for a winning variation, leading to the deployment of features that don't actually move the needle.

info

Pro Tip

Our AI models typically require at least 24 hours of data collection to reach a stable 95% confidence interval, even for high-traffic sites.

Reading the Confidence Graph

The dashboard presents a visual representation of the probability density for each variation. The narrower the curve, the more confident the model is about the conversion rate.

Threshold Configuration

You can adjust the sensitivity of the AI decision engine in your project settings. Lowering the p-value threshold increases strictness but requires more traffic.

const config = {
  significanceLevel: 0.05, // 95% Confidence
  minSampleHeader: 1000,
  autoStop: true
};

Interpreting P-Values

A p-value measures the probability that the observed difference between variation A and B occurred by random chance. In the A/B AI platform, we highlight p-values below 0.05 in blue to indicate statistical significance.
