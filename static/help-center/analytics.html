Overview

Samplit uses Bayesian statistics to analyze your experiment results. This approach provides faster insights, probability-based decisions, and expected loss calculations, allowing you to adapt without needing fixed sample sizes.

The Results Dashboard

The results dashboard is designed to give you a clear, at-a-glance view of your experiment's progress and potential winners.

Key Metrics Explained

  • Visitors: The number of unique visitors who saw each variation.
  • Conversions: Total unique users who completed your primary goal.
  • Conversion Rate: Percentage of visitors who converted (Conversions / Visitors).
  • Confidence: The probability that this variation outperforms the control.
  • Lift: Percentage improvement compared to the control version.

Bayesian Statistics in Samplit

Unlike traditional (Frequentist) statistics which focus on p-values and significance, Samplit's Bayesian engine calculates the Probability of Being Best. We analyze the entire distribution of possible conversion rates for each variation, providing a more intuitive and actionable decision framework.

Expected Loss

Expected loss tells you exactly what you risk losing (in conversion rate) if you implement a variant and it turns out not to be the true winner. High confidence + low expected loss = a safe decision.

Reading the Confidence Score

Confidence Meaning Action
< 75% No clear winner yet Continue testing
75-90% Promising results Monitor closely
95% High certainty Recommend implementation
99%+ Almost certain winner Full implementation

Segment Analysis

Don't just look at the overall average. Breakdown your results to find deeper insights:

  • Device: Compare mobile vs. desktop performance.
  • Geography: See how different regions respond to your changes.
  • Source: Analyze organic vs. paid traffic behavior.
