# Copy Testing: The Most Underrated Conversion Lever

Most teams obsess over button colors and page layouts while ignoring the element that drives 80% of conversion decisions: **copy**.

A headline change can double your conversion rate. A CTA tweak can add 30% to your revenue. Yet most A/B testing tools make copy testing unnecessarily complex.

## Why Copy Matters More Than Design

Research from Nielsen Norman Group shows that users spend 80% of their time reading text, not looking at images or design elements.

Your copy does three things:
1. **Captures attention** (headline)
2. **Builds trust** (value proposition)
3. **Drives action** (CTA)

Change any of these, and you change conversion rates.

## What to Test First

### 1. Headlines

Your headline is read 5x more than your body copy. Test:
- Emotional vs. rational appeals
- Benefit-focused vs. feature-focused
- Question vs. statement format

**Example:**
```
Control: "A/B Testing Software for Modern Teams"
Variant: "Stop Guessing. Start Testing."
Result: +42% click-through rate
```

### 2. Call-to-Action (CTA)

The CTA is where conversion happens. Test:
- Commitment level ("Try Free" vs. "Get Started" vs. "Sign Up")
- Action clarity ("See Demo" vs. "Watch Video")
- Urgency ("Start Now" vs. "Start Today")

### 3. Value Proposition

This is your "why should I care?" statement. Test:
- Outcome-focused vs. process-focused
- Specific vs. general claims
- Social proof vs. authority

## How to Generate Variants

Don't just change words randomly. Use frameworks:

### The 4 U's Framework
- **Urgent**: "Limited spots available"
- **Unique**: "The only tool that..."
- **Useful**: "Save 10 hours per week"
- **Ultra-specific**: "Increase conversions by 23%"

### Voice of Customer
Mine reviews, support tickets, and sales calls for exact phrases customers use. They often write better copy than you.

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Testing Too Many Changes
Test one element at a time. If you change both headline and CTA, you won't know which drove the result.

### 2. Ignoring Context
A headline that works on a landing page might fail in an email. Context matters.

### 3. Stopping Too Early
Copy tests often show smaller effect sizes than design changes. You need more traffic to reach significance.

## Measuring Copy Performance

Track these metrics:
- **Click-through rate** (for headlines)
- **Time on page** (for body copy)
- **Conversion rate** (for CTAs)
- **Bounce rate** (for relevance)

We tested three headlines on our own homepage:

**Why it worked:** Direct benefit, action-oriented, no jargon.
